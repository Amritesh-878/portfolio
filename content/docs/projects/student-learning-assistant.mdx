## What it is

A production retrieval assistant at Impact Solutions Lab that gives each student a chatbot grounded
in **their own classes**. Recordings come in, are transcribed, and indexed per student, and the
material each class was taught from is indexed alongside them. A student can ask what a class
covered or ask about a concept, and the answer draws only on their own sessions and their class's
material, and says which of the two it came from.

Because this system handles students' audio, this page shows the system design in the abstract and
nothing else. No recordings, no transcripts, no student data.

## System design

<ClassRecordingPipeline />

- **Dual-language transcription.** Class audio is transcribed with WhisperX, choosing the stronger
  of two languages per segment, with silence filtered to avoid hallucinated text.
- **Two grounding sources, attributed.** The spoken transcript answers "what happened in class";
  the class's own teaching material answers concept questions. Every answer states which source it
  drew from, and the assistant declines when neither covers the question rather than improvising.
- **Per-student isolation.** Each student's content is indexed under their own scope, and retrieval
  is gated so an answer can only ever draw from that student's own classes. This boundary is
  enforced in the pipeline, not left to a prompt.
- **Automated ingestion.** New recordings and materials are picked up on a schedule and processed
  idempotently, so the same session is never indexed twice.
